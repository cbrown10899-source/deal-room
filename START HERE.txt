DEAL ROOM — start here
=======================

A private little app for vetting business deals against the checklist that
prominent family offices use — capital preservation first, verify before you
trust, separate real evidence from salesmanship.

The best part: it doesn't need any account, sign-up, or paid key. The scoring is
done by YOUR OWN CLAUDE. Deal Room organizes the deal and hands it to Claude with
the right instructions; Claude reads the file and scores it for you.

FASTEST WAY IN — it's already online:

    https://cbrown10899-source.github.io/deal-room/

Just open that link. On your phone, then tap "Add to Home Screen" and it installs
like a real app. Nothing to download, and it's just as private (see below).

You also got two files, in case you'd rather keep it as a file or host it yourself:
  • "Deal Room.html"  — the app itself. This IS the whole program.
  • "START HERE.txt"  — this note.


OPENING IT — pick whatever's handy
----------------------------------
ON YOUR PHONE (keep it on your home screen like an app):
  • Open the link above in Safari (iPhone) or Chrome (Android), then use
    "Add to Home Screen" (Share menu on iPhone; the ⋮ menu on Android). It
    launches full-screen like a real app.

ON YOUR COMPUTER:
  • Open the link above, or double-click "Deal Room.html" to run it offline.
  • Use Chrome, Edge, or Safari in a NORMAL (not private/incognito) window, so it
    can save your deals.

INSIDE CLAUDE:
  • You can also drop "Deal Room.html" into a Claude chat and say "open this Deal
    Room app in your browser." Claude will run it for you right there.

FROM YOUR OWN WEBSITE (the most private, and the best phone install):
  • Put this one file on a website you control and open that link. Upload it to a
    free host like Cloudflare Pages, Netlify, or GitHub Pages, or drop it in a
    spare folder on a site/domain you already own.
  • Open your link, then "Add to Home Screen" — it installs cleanly and lives at
    your own address. Keep the link unlisted or password-protect it, and it's
    yours alone.
  • It stays just as private: the page is plain static HTML with no tracking and
    no accounts, your deals and files never leave your browser, and the only thing
    ever sent out is the file YOU choose to hand to your own Claude.

Your deals live only in the browser you use, on that device. Now and then use
Settings -> "Export all deals" to keep a backup file.


HOW TO VET A DEAL (about 3 taps)
--------------------------------
1. Tap "+ New deal", give it a name, and pick the type. (The type tunes the
   scorecard weighting — startups lean on the people, real estate on the terms.)

2. DROP THE RAISE FILES. Drag the pitch deck / financials / term sheet onto the
   drop zone (or tap it to choose). PDFs, images, spreadsheets — whatever they
   sent you.

3. TAP "Copy instructions & open Claude." This copies a full family-office
   analyst brief and opens Claude. In Claude:
     - attach the same raise file(s), and
     - paste (the instructions are already on your clipboard).
   Claude reads the file and gives you a scored report: a summary, the key facts,
   a 1-to-5 score with the reasoning for each thing it could judge, the red-flag
   deal-killers it spotted, what's missing, and the questions to ask the sponsor.
   If it needs more to score something, it'll tell you exactly what to send — just
   attach it and Claude revises.

4. (Optional) SAVE THE RESULT. Copy Claude's reply, paste it back into the app's
   "Save the result" box, and Deal Room keeps the scored report and an A-F grade
   with the deal. (Claude ends its reply with a little data block the app reads —
   just paste the whole thing.)

That's it. No key, no cost beyond your normal Claude, nothing leaves your device
except the file you choose to hand to your own Claude.


THE ONE RULE WORTH KNOWING
--------------------------
Any single "deal-killer" red flag (guaranteed returns, pressure to close fast,
financials that don't reconcile, a sponsor who can't explain how the money is
made...) caps the grade at 49 and pushes the verdict to PASS — no matter how good
the rest of the story sounds. That's the family-office reflex, and it's the whole
point of vetting before you wire a dollar.
